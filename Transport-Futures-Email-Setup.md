**How it works (the simple version)**
Instead of a brand-new account to log into, your TF address connects to the email you already use. You'll receive mail sent to your TF address right in your **normal inbox**, and when you reply, recipients see it coming **from your `@transportfutures.org` address**. No new password to remember, nothing extra to check each day.

**Why we're doing it this way for now**
While we're a lean team, this approach is quick, free, and keeps things simple — everyone stays in the inbox they already know. As the team and our work grow, we'll move to full standalone mailboxes (each with its own login). For now, this gives us the professional address without the extra overhead.

## Set it up in Gmail (about 3 minutes)

1. In Gmail, click the gear icon → **See all settings** → **Accounts and Import** tab.
2. Under **"Send mail as,"** click **Add another email address**.
3. **Name:** your full name. **Email address:** your TF address (from the table above). Leave **"Treat as an alias" ticked**. Click **Next Step**.
4. On the SMTP screen, **overwrite the auto-filled values** with exactly these:
   - SMTP Server: smtp-relay.brevo.com
   - Port: 587
   - Username: ad0a97001@smtp-brevo.com
   - Password: (the setup key I send you)
   - Select **Secured connection using TLS**, then click **Add Account**.
5. Gmail emails a **confirmation code** to your TF address — it lands in your normal inbox. Open it and either click the link or paste the code, then **Verify**.
6. Back in **Accounts and Import**, turn on **"Reply from the same address the message was sent to."**

Done. Now when you reply to someone who wrote to your TF address, it goes out as your @transportfutures.org address.

*Optional:* create a filter (To: your TF address → apply a label) to keep TF mail neatly separated from personal mail.

The steps above are for Gmail. If you use Outlook or another mail app (e.g. an institutional account), the idea is identical — add a "send as / send on behalf" identity using the same SMTP settings above — but the menus differ. Message me and I'll send tailored steps.
